WHO WE ARE


Africa Education Holding (AEH) is a tertiary education company

that provides accessible and transformative education in Sub Saharan Africa

The Problem

The core challenge for Africa’s youth is:

  • Securing gainful employment or entrepreneurship.
  • Limited access to high quality, market relevant tertiary education.
  • Constrained mindset shaped by adverse social-economic and cultural conditions.
  • Scarce opportunities for gainful employment & entrepreneurship in Sub-Saharan Africa.

The Opportunity for Transformation

Leveraging Africa's Dynamic Potential

  • Africa's Youth: A large, digitally native, and entrepreneurial demographic.
  • Technology & Innovation: Tools to enhance education efficiency and foster new enterprises.
  • Globalization: Access to global markets, education platforms, and mobility for talent.
  • Transformative Impact: Potential to drive economic and intellectual growth across the continent.

OUR PURPOSE

We are building more than a university.

We are pioneering a New Model of an African university – one designed to prepare and help each student to achieve gainful employment and entrepreneurship on a global scale, unlocking Africa’s vast economic potential


This is a scalable model that anchors our mission to provide accessible, market relevant and transformative education to Africa’s youth

OUR SOLUTION

We are addressing Africa’s youth employment crisis through a student-centered model built on three pillars

  • Holistic Education for the African Student: Our universities nurture identity, values, and essential life skills to develop grounded purpose driven graduates
  • Apprenticeship-Driven, Skills-Based Learning: We emphasize learning by doing –through real-world industry experience, innovation, and collaboration
  • Pathways to Global Employment and Entrepreneurship: We connect students to local and global opportunities across employment and entrepreneurship landscapes

Our Universities


AEH owns and operates four private universities in Uganda and Zambia

(Anglophone) and Senegal and Mali (Francophone) with a combined Total

Enrolment of 14,500 students
